What this page is saying

Crowding re-scales the knobs

Every rate constant becomes a function of crowder fraction C. Excluded volume makes molecules act more concentrated, so binding and association speed up (k₁, k₂ up, Km,P down). Viscosity slows diffusion, dragging the same association rates back down. Crowding also shifts the polymerase and exonuclease turnover.

The tug-of-war

Excluded volume speeds reactions up; viscosity slows them down. They push the period in opposite directions, so the net effect is not obvious in advance. That is exactly why a model is worth building: it turns your assumed effect strengths into a concrete, testable curve.

The strongest possible result

Watch the sweep plot. If raising C carries the amplitude across zero, then crowding switches the oscillation on or off (it crosses the Hopf boundary). Showing that crowding alone can start or stop the rhythm is the cleanest, most striking claim you can make, and it is fully falsifiable.

Be honest about the unknowns

The effect-strength sliders are hypotheses, not measured values. The biggest unknown is how crowding affects the exonuclease (ttRecJ), since the oscillation hinges on it. Wet lab measures these by running the enzyme assays in crowder; this model just shows what each assumption predicts.

⚠ Defaults are illustrative. Replace the effect strengths with values fitted to crowding literature or your own calibration runs before treating any curve as a real prediction.
